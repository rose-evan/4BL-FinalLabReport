# CLAUDE.md — Agent Context for 4BL Final Lab Report

## Project Type
This is an **undergraduate college lab report** for Physics 4BL.
- It is NOT a graduate-level paper
- It is a **lab report**, not a research paper
- Writing should be accessible to someone with a freshman physics background
- Past tense throughout

## Grading Rubric

### Title, Author, and Abstract (10 pts)

**Title (3 pts):**
- (1 pt) Placed at the top of the report
- (1 pt) Specific, concise, and descriptive
- (1 pt) Clearly communicates the physical system, method, and/or quantity being investigated

**Authors (2 pts):**
- (1 pt) Full names of all group members
- (1 pt) Group number included

**Abstract (5 pts)** — standalone summary (150–200 words):
- (1 pt) Purpose and significance of the experiment
- (1 pt) Hypothesis or expected outcome
- (1 pt) Brief description of the method
- (1 pt) Final quantitative results with uncertainties
- (1 pt) Conclusion comparing results to the hypothesis

---

### Introduction/Theory (15 pts)
- (4 pts) Provides sufficient background physics to motivate the experiment, defining all non-introductory-level concepts (e.g., bandgap)
- (4 pts) Includes all relevant working equations without lengthy derivations:
  - Variables are defined
  - Assumptions and physical laws are stated
- (4 pts) Clearly answers:
  - Why is this experiment important?
  - What physical principles are being tested or applied?
- (3 pts) Written at a level accessible to someone with a freshman physics background

---

### Methods/Experimental Setup (25 pts)
- (15 pts) Describes what was done, including:
  - Description of apparatus and major components
  - Experimental procedure (including the calibration)
  - Controlled and measured variables
- (5 pts) Sufficient detail for reproducibility by a freshman physics student unfamiliar with the experiment
- (5 pts) Contains at least one clearly labeled diagram or photograph of the setup
- Avoid unnecessary procedural detail (this is not a lab manual)

---

### Results and Analysis (25 pts)
- (5 pts) Present processed data (tables and plots)
- (4 pts) All tables and plots should have proper labels, units, and captions
- (4 pts) Describe data processing and analysis methods (e.g., fitting, averaging, uncertainty propagation), including why each step was performed and what was learned
- (4 pts) Results are interpreted:
  - Trends and patterns are explained
  - Comparison to theoretical predictions or hypotheses is made
- (4 pts) All numerical results should include uncertainties
- (4 pts) Discrepancies between expected and observed results are discussed with plausible explanations

---

### Conclusion (15 pts)
- (3 pts) Summarizes key findings concisely
- (3 pts) Explicitly states whether the hypothesis was supported
- (3 pts) Uses quantitative results (from the previous section) to justify conclusions
- (3 pts) Identifies dominant sources of error and their impact
- (3 pts) Discusses possible improvements or extensions of the experiment

---

### Format (10 pts)
- Single-spaced, well-organized, and easy to read
- Written primarily in past tense
- Figures and tables:
  - Integrated smoothly into the text
  - Properly numbered and captioned
  - Referenced explicitly in the text (e.g., "As shown in Figure 2…")
- Axes, units, scales, and symbols are legible and clearly labeled
- Visuals are consistent in style and not redundant
- All figures, plots, and text are original unless explicitly cited
- External sources (including the lab slides) are properly referenced in captions and bibliography
